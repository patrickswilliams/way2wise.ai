The EQ Agent

An external mirror for your inner work.

"EQ" is emotional intelligence — the awareness and self-control that technical training skips over. Getting the job takes technical skill. Managing frustration, staying focused under pressure, and communicating honestly is what carries a career. The EQ Agent guides short weekly reflections and turns them into personalized insight.

Evidence first

Born from research, not a pitch deck.

The EQ Agent began as a graduate research study at Pepperdine University, examining how personalized AI interactions reveal emotional intelligence in working software engineers.

Qualitative case study

A month-long intervention with practicing engineers, triangulating AI reflection logs, weekly entries, and in-depth interviews.

University-approved methods

Conducted under Pepperdine University's research-ethics review, with informed consent and full anonymization throughout.

Privacy by design

The system is built so personal information never enters it, access is tightly limited, and every AI output can be traced and audited.

Fits what you already do

It works with your learning, not around it.

The EQ Agent isn't another course to schedule or platform to migrate to. It's a thin layer of guided reflection that wraps around the technical training, programs, and real projects your engineers are already engaged in.

Maps to your curriculum

Weekly themes align to recognized emotional-intelligence skills, so they slot into existing soft-skill and leadership tracks in your learning platform or course catalog — onboarding, capstones, promotion readiness, tech-lead transitions.

Turns experience into learning

Reflections draw on the engineer's actual week — a tense code review, a missed estimate, a good pairing session — so growth compounds with the work they're doing rather than sitting in a separate silo.

Amplifies what's working

It pairs naturally with mentorship, agile and team-health practices, and existing leadership development, giving those efforts a consistent, personal feedback loop between sessions.

Why way2wise exists

I had to learn this the hard way.

Patrick Williams
Founder & Chief EQ Architect
  • Nearly two decades at Google — Learning & Development, SMB tools, compensation systems
  • Earlier career across IBM, startups, and a venture acquired by Google
  • Graduate research on EQ and AI at Pepperdine University
  • Operating principle: Assess · Flex · Partner

way2wise.ai grew out of a simple conviction: the skills that decide an engineer's career are rarely the ones their training focuses on. I know this firsthand, because the human side of work is the part I had to fight for.

I grew up on Navy bases, the son and grandson of Rear Admirals — a world of dedication and constant upheaval that taught me to read a room and adapt fast. School was harder. For years I believed I was slow or lazy, until a dyslexia diagnosis gave the struggle a name and a patient teacher gave it a path forward.

"That's what the EQ Agent is — the patient mirror I wish more people had access to."

A career across IBM, startups, and nearly two decades at Google taught me that confidence is built one hard experience at a time. A 2015 bipolar diagnosis reframed the rest: the energy and self-doubt finally made sense, and self-regulation became a discipline I had to learn on purpose. That lived experience is the foundation the EQ Agent is built on — and the reason I'm building way2wise.ai for the next generation of engineers.
